Essential Job Search Tips for Filipino Students & Fresh Graduates

Finding your first job or internship can be challenging, but with these practical tips, you can successfully launch your career in the Philippines:

Target the Right Opportunities

  • Apply for roles that match your actual skills and qualifications
  • Don't limit yourself to just your degree field - consider adjacent industries
  • Research growing sectors in the Philippines (IT, BPO, E-commerce) for better prospects
  • Consider internships or volunteering to build experience before applying for full-time roles
  • Look for "fresh graduate" or "entry-level" tags in job listings

Timing Your Applications

  • Apply to jobs posted within the last week for better response rates
  • Many companies hire at the beginning of the year (January-March) and after midyear (July-September)
  • Set up job alerts on platforms like JobStreet and Indeed to be notified of new opportunities
  • Multinational companies often recruit fresh graduates in batches (check their career pages for schedules)
  • Don't wait until after graduation - start applying during your final semester

Filipino Workplace Culture Tips

  • Understand Filipino workplace expectations: punctuality, respect for hierarchy, and strong team orientation
  • Develop your English communication skills (both written and verbal)
  • Practice adaptability and resilience ("madiskarte" mindset)
  • Emphasize your ability to work with diverse teams
  • Learn basic business Tagalog for workplaces that operate in both languages

Industry-Specific Tips for Filipino Graduates

IT & BPO Industries

High Demand
  • Create a GitHub portfolio to showcase coding projects
  • Earn relevant certifications (AWS, CompTIA, CCNA)
  • Participate in hackathons and coding competitions
  • Join tech communities like Developer Student Clubs
  • Develop your English communication skills

Business & Finance

Competitive
  • Stay updated on Philippine economic news
  • Consider certification exams like CPA or CFA
  • Develop Excel and financial modeling skills
  • Join organizations like JCI Philippines
  • Highlight analytical abilities in applications

Hospitality & Tourism

Recovering
  • Highlight any customer service experience
  • Develop multilingual skills (English, Asian languages)
  • Get certified in food safety if relevant
  • Show flexibility with scheduling (weekends, holidays)
  • Develop knowledge of Philippine tourist destinations

Creative & Media

Portfolio-Based
  • Create a professional portfolio website
  • Build profiles on Behance, Dribbble, or YouTube
  • Learn industry-standard software (Adobe Suite)
  • Network with agencies via social media
  • Take on freelance projects to build experience

Healthcare & Medical

Essential
  • Keep licenses and registrations current (PRC)
  • Consider specialized certifications (BLS, ACLS)
  • Join healthcare professional associations
  • Look for hospital training programs for fresh grads
  • Consider telemedicine opportunities (growing field)

Education & Teaching

Stable
  • Obtain necessary teaching credentials (LET)
  • Develop both online and in-person teaching skills
  • Create a teaching portfolio with sample lessons
  • Consider ESL certification for more opportunities
  • Volunteer as a tutor to gain experience
