CV & Resume Creation Guide

Learn how to create effective CVs and resumes specifically designed for the Philippine job market.

CV Content Checklist

Essential elements to include in your CV:

  • Contact information with professional email
  • Educational background with relevant coursework
  • Skills section highlighting technical and soft skills
  • Internship and OJT experience
  • Extracurricular activities and leadership roles

ATS-Friendly Formatting

Format your CV to pass applicant tracking systems:

  • Use standard section headings
  • Incorporate keywords from job descriptions
  • Use simple, readable fonts
  • Save as PDF unless otherwise specified
  • Avoid complex formatting and graphics

Philippine Context Tips

Localized advice for the Philippine job market:

  • Include your photo (ID-style) in the upper right
  • List character references when appropriate
  • Mention language proficiencies (Filipino, English, etc.)
  • Include relevant seminars and trainings
  • Mention willingness to work flexible hours if applicable

Achievement Statements

How to write impressive achievements with limited experience:

  • Use action verbs (developed, created, managed)
  • Add metrics where possible (improved by 25%, managed team of 5)
  • Focus on outcomes, not just responsibilities
  • Highlight academic achievements when work experience is limited
  • Quantify volunteer and extracurricular accomplishments

Interview Preparation

Prepare for internship and entry-level interviews with our comprehensive resources.

Common Interview Questions

Practice your answers to these frequently asked questions for fresh graduates in the Philippines:

  1. Tell me about yourself.
    Focus on your education, relevant experiences, and career aspirations.
  2. Why did you choose your course/degree?
    Explain your passion and interests that led to your educational choices.
  3. What are your strengths and weaknesses?
    Highlight strengths relevant to the role and mention weaknesses you're actively working to improve.
  4. Why do you want to work for our company?
    Research the company thoroughly and connect your goals with their mission and values.
  5. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
    Outline realistic career progression within the company or industry.
  6. How do you handle pressure and stress?
    Share specific examples from school projects or internships.
  7. How would you contribute to our company?
    Connect your skills and experiences to their specific needs.
  8. How do you work in a team?
    Provide examples from group projects or extracurricular activities.
  9. What is your expected salary?
    Research industry standards for entry-level positions in your field.
  10. Do you have any questions for us?
    Always prepare thoughtful questions about the role, company culture, or growth opportunities.

Online Interview Tips

Practical advice for virtual interviews:

  • Test your technology beforehand (camera, microphone, internet)
  • Choose a quiet, well-lit location with a professional background
  • Dress professionally from head to toe
  • Have your resume and notes nearby but don't constantly look at them
  • Maintain eye contact by looking at the camera

Interview Day Checklist

Items to prepare before your interview:

  • Multiple copies of your resume
  • Portfolio or work samples (if applicable)
  • List of references with contact information
  • Notebook and pen for taking notes
  • Questions prepared for the interviewer

Networking for Beginners

Build your professional connections from scratch with these practical networking strategies.

Alumni Connections

How to leverage your school's alumni network:

  • Join your university's alumni association
  • Attend alumni networking events (virtual or in-person)
  • Connect with alumni on LinkedIn working in your target industry
  • Request informational interviews with alumni in relevant roles
  • Participate in alumni mentorship programs

LinkedIn Optimization

Enhance your LinkedIn profile for Filipino recruiters:

  • Use a professional headshot (not a selfie)
  • Create a compelling headline that includes your degree and career focus
  • Highlight internships, academic achievements, and extracurriculars
  • Join industry-specific groups related to the Philippine job market
  • Connect with recruiters from companies you're interested in

Informational Interviews

How to request and conduct informational interviews:

  • Research professionals in your target role/industry
  • Craft a polite, specific request explaining why you'd like to connect
  • Prepare thoughtful questions about their career path and industry
  • Keep the meeting brief (15-30 minutes) and respect their time
  • Send a thank-you note afterward and maintain the relationship

Industry Groups

Professional associations for Filipino students and graduates:

  • Philippine Society of IT Professionals (PSITE)
  • Philippine Marketing Association (PMA)
  • Financial Executives Institute of the Philippines (FINEX)
  • Philippine Institute of Civil Engineers (PICE)
  • Junior Chamber International (JCI) Philippines
