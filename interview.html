Interview Preparation Tips

Follow these expert tips to thoroughly prepare for your upcoming interview.

Before the Interview

Proper preparation is key to interview success. Here's how to make sure you're ready:

  • Research the Company

    Learn about the company's mission, values, products/services, recent news, and challenges. Visit their website, social media profiles, and read recent press releases.

  • Study the Job Description

    Analyze the job requirements and prepare examples from your experience that demonstrate relevant skills and achievements.

  • Prepare Your Answers

    Practice responses to common interview questions, focusing on clear, concise answers with specific examples.

  • Develop Questions to Ask

    Prepare 3-5 thoughtful questions about the role, team, company culture, and growth opportunities to show your genuine interest.

  • Plan Your Appearance

    Select professional attire appropriate for the company's culture, ensuring everything is clean, ironed, and fits well.

  • Prepare Your Materials

    Bring multiple copies of your resume, a notepad, pen, portfolio if applicable, and a list of references.

  • Practice Interview Techniques

    Conduct mock interviews with a friend or mentor, or practice in front of a mirror to improve your delivery and body language.

Interview Do's and Don'ts

Follow these guidelines to make a positive impression during your interview.

Professional Behavior During Interviews

Do Don't
Arrive 10-15 minutes early Show up late or too early (30+ minutes)
Make eye contact and offer a firm handshake Avoid eye contact or give a weak handshake
Listen carefully and ask for clarification if needed Interrupt the interviewer
Demonstrate positive body language (sit up straight, nod) Fidget, slouch, or cross arms defensively
Provide specific examples of your achievements Give vague or generic answers
Speak positively about previous employers Criticize or complain about past jobs/bosses
Show enthusiasm for the role and company Appear disinterested or overly casual
Turn off your phone before entering Check your phone during the interview
Ask thoughtful questions about the role and company Focus only on salary, benefits, or time off
Thank the interviewer and follow up with a thank-you email Leave without expressing appreciation for their time

Interview Etiquette for Virtual Interviews

  • Test Your Technology

    Ensure your camera, microphone, and internet connection work properly before the interview.

  • Create a Professional Environment

    Choose a quiet, well-lit location with a clean, neutral background. Minimize potential interruptions.

  • Dress Professionally

    Wear complete professional attire as you would for an in-person interview, not just from the waist up.

  • Look at the Camera

    Make "eye contact" by looking at your camera rather than the screen when speaking.

Common Interview Questions & How to Answer

Prepare for these frequently asked questions with our expert advice on structuring effective responses.

Mastering Interview Questions

Click on each question to see strategies for crafting impressive answers:

Tell me about yourself.

This is often the opening question and is your opportunity to make a strong first impression. The interviewer wants an overview of your professional background and what brings you to this role.

Answer Strategy:

Structure your answer in three parts:

  1. Present: Start with your current role and key responsibilities
  2. Past: Briefly mention relevant previous experience and achievements
  3. Future: Connect your background to why you're interested in this position

Keep your answer focused on professional information relevant to the job (1-2 minutes long). Avoid oversharing personal details.

What are your greatest strengths?

Employers want to know what you excel at and how your strengths align with the position. This question assesses your self-awareness and how well you understand the job requirements.

Answer Strategy:

Select 2-3 strengths that are:

  • Directly relevant to the job requirements
  • Verifiable with specific examples
  • A mix of technical and soft skills

For each strength, provide a brief STAR-format example (Situation, Task, Action, Result) that demonstrates how you've used this strength successfully.

What is your greatest weakness?

This question tests your self-awareness, honesty, and commitment to professional growth. Employers want to see that you can acknowledge areas for improvement.

Answer Strategy:

Choose a genuine but not critical weakness, and focus on:

  1. Acknowledge: Identify an actual skill or trait you're working to improve
  2. Explain: Briefly discuss how this affects your work
  3. Improve: Detail specific steps you're taking to overcome this weakness
  4. Results: Mention progress you've already made

Avoid clichés like "I'm a perfectionist" or weaknesses that are essential to the job.

Why do you want to work for this company?

This question evaluates whether you've researched the company and how well you understand their culture, values, and industry position.

Answer Strategy:

Demonstrate genuine interest by:

  • Mentioning specific aspects of the company's mission or values that resonate with you
  • Referencing recent company achievements, projects, or innovations that impress you
  • Explaining how the company's work environment or culture aligns with your career goals
  • Connecting your skills and interests to the company's needs

Make it clear why this specific company stands out to you compared to others in the industry.

Why should we hire you?

This question is your opportunity to sell yourself and highlight what makes you the best candidate. The interviewer wants to understand the unique value you bring.

Answer Strategy:

Create a compelling case by:

  1. Match: Highlight how your skills and experience align perfectly with the job requirements
  2. Results: Share specific achievements that demonstrate your impact
  3. Unique: Emphasize what distinguishes you from other candidates
  4. Cultural: Explain why you're a good fit for the company culture

Be confident but not arrogant. Focus on concrete evidence of your capabilities rather than making unsupported claims.

Can you describe a challenging work situation and how you handled it?

Behavioral questions like this assess how you've responded to difficulties in the past, which helps predict how you'll perform in future challenging situations.

Answer Strategy:

Use the STAR method to structure your answer:

  • Situation: Briefly describe the context and specific challenge
  • Task: Explain your responsibilities in addressing the challenge
  • Action: Detail the specific steps you took to resolve the issue
  • Result: Share the positive outcomes and what you learned

Choose an example that showcases valuable skills like problem-solving, communication, leadership, or adaptability. Focus more on the solution than the problem.

Where do you see yourself in five years?

This question evaluates your career ambitions, commitment to the role/company, and whether your goals align with what the organization can offer.

Answer Strategy:

Balance ambition with realism by:

  • Showing genuine interest in growing with the company long-term
  • Outlining realistic advancement goals within the company's structure
  • Emphasizing skills you hope to develop or master
  • Connecting your aspirations to the company's mission or growth

Avoid responses that suggest you see the position as a short-term stepping stone or have unrealistic expectations about advancement.

What are your salary expectations?

This question helps employers determine if your expectations align with their budget. It also tests your knowledge of industry standards and your own value.

Answer Strategy:

Approach this question strategically:

  1. Research: Before the interview, research salary ranges for similar positions in your location
  2. Range: Provide a reasonable range rather than a specific number
  3. Flexibility: Express openness to discussing compensation
  4. Total package: Mention that you're considering the entire benefits package

If possible, try to delay detailed salary discussions until you've received an offer or have fully demonstrated your value.
